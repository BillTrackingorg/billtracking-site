Terms of use

The deal, in plain words

BillTracking republishes the official legislative record of the US Congress and the European Union. It is free, there is nothing to buy, and it is run by one person, not a company. These terms are short because the arrangement is simple.

What this is

What you're getting

Automated feeds and an app that report steps in the legislative process, taken from official sources — Congress.gov, the European Parliament's open data, the Publications Office and the OEIL Legislative Observatory. Plain-English summaries are AI-written and labelled [AI-Generated]. Procedural facts, dates and vote tallies are copied from the record and are not AI-written.

This is not legal advice, and it is not the official record. It is a convenience layer over public sources. Every post links to its source. If a decision matters — legally, professionally, financially — read the source, not us.

It will contain errors

Automatically classifying legislative stages is genuinely difficult, and the record itself is sometimes amended after the fact. We do not promise accuracy; we promise that errors are corrected in the open and logged publicly. See Accuracy & corrections, and please report anything that looks wrong to accuracy@billtracking.org.

No guarantee it keeps running

It is a free service run by one person on free infrastructure. It may be interrupted, delayed, or discontinued. If an upstream source changes or goes down, the feeds may go quiet. Please do not build anything you depend on around its continued availability without talking to us first.

Accounts and voting

If you sign in and vote

  • Reading never requires an account. An account exists so that a vote on a bill counts once per person.
  • One account per person. Do not create multiple accounts to vote more than once, and do not use automated means to influence the counts. It corrupts the only thing the counts are for.
  • Votes are reader sentiment, not a poll. They are a show of hands among whoever happens to use this app — self-selected, unweighted and unrepresentative. Please do not present them as public opinion research, and be careful citing them as anything more than what they are.
  • You may withdraw a vote or delete your account at any time, from the You tab or via the deletion page.
  • We may remove votes we believe are automated or duplicated, and may suspend an account doing it.

How votes are stored and published is set out on the privacy page. In short: who voted which way is never published, shown or attached to a profile.

Using our stuff, and us using yours

Content and reuse

The underlying legislative record is public and is not ours. US federal government works are not subject to copyright (17 U.S.C. § 105); EU documents are reusable under the European Commission's reuse decision and the European Parliament's reuse notice. Where you need the authoritative version, go to api.congress.gov, the OEIL Legislative Observatory, or the EU Publications Office directly.

Our own writing, design and code are ours. Teachers and students: use it. Quote it, screenshot it, put it in a lecture, build a class exercise on it — that is what it is for, and there is a page of ready-made exercises. A credit and a link is all we ask. If you want a bulk dataset, write to us rather than scraping.

Do not use the service to break the law, to attack the infrastructure, or to misrepresent what it says.
